But Mom You Promised...

These words can wreck havoc to your heart. Here I was thinking "I need to finish this report, before I take them to the mall, I need a clone" . One hour later as I quickly hit send and hope the report will make sense to the recipient, I see my kids look of disappointment and my heart sinks. It can be difficult for children to understand when a promise is broken, especially if they are young and may not have a full understanding of the complexities of life, I work so I can put food on the table and be able to take you to the mall. Here are some ways to make them feel okay and you avoid emotionally damaging them. Apologize: It's important to acknowledge when you have let your child down, even if it was not intentional. A sincere apology can go a long way in helping a child feel understood and validated. Encourage the child to express their feelings and ask questions about the broken promise. This can help them better understand the situation and feel like they have a say in the matter.
Where there is a will, there is a way.
